# Project: Just Play Bologna

The website of Just Play Bologna — a non-profit, Arci-affiliated board-game association in Bologna, Italy. The club hosts board games, role-playing games, chess, and Subbuteo nights at the Casa del Popolo "Bruno Tosarelli" in the Corticella neighborhood. All copy is in Italian.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary: a curious newcomer.** Someone in Bologna who has heard about the club, or stumbled on the site, and is deciding whether to show up on Monday at 9pm. They've never been. They don't know if they need to book, what to wear, whether they need an Arci card, whether they'll be welcome if they don't already know boardgames. The whole interface needs to answer the unspoken question: *"will I feel out of place?"* — and the answer must be a clear *no*.

Active members (checking the game catalog on Google Sheets, looking up rules PDFs) and external hobbyists are secondary. The design vocabulary is tuned for first-impression conversion of a stranger, not for power-user efficiency.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **warm, hand-touched, neighborly.**

The voice is a friend telling you about their favorite weekly hangout — not an institution communicating its services. The site should feel like the same volunteers who run the club also made it: a little uneven, full of personality, undeniably non-corporate.

The emotional target is *lowering the threshold of arrival* — the silent reassurance that strangers belong here. Risograph community-zine, neighborhood bulletin board, weeknight café-with-board-games, Casa del Popolo poster wall. Not gamer-portal, not SaaS, not Material Design.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Warm community zine** with an Arci-red anchor.

- Generous warm neutrals (cream paper, ink-brown), not Material grays. Pure white and pure black are banned.
- Hand-touched details: slight irregularities, illustrated/iconographic accents over icon-font glyphs, organic shapes where geometry would feel too clinical. Texture is welcome — paper grain, halftone, Risograph misregister, Italian poster offset. Used sparingly so the site still loads fast.
- Type pairing should mix a characterful **display** face (zine-y, expressive, even slightly chunky) with a **humanist body** face that handles long Italian copy (the FAQ is dense). Avoid every face on the impeccable reflex-rejection list (no Inter, IBM Plex, DM Sans, Fraunces, Cormorant, Playfair, Lora, Crimson, Outfit, Plus Jakarta, Instrument, Space, Syne, Newsreader, Cabin). Look further afield — candidates like **Alegreya / Alegreya Sans**, **Bricolage Grotesque**, **Caprasimo**, **Funnel Display**, **Eczar**, **Inria Serif**, or fonts from Velvetyne / Pangram Pangram tend to fit.
- Italian-design lineage: this site lives in Italy, made by Italians, for Italians. Reference points are *Italian* community-cultural design (Casa del Popolo posters, Italian neighborhood-festa flyers, ARCI publications, mid-century Italian board game boxes), not US SaaS templates.

**Anti-references:** generic Material 2018 (which is what the site looks like today — Roboto + indigo-pink), gamer-portal dark UIs with neon accents, glassmorphism, gradient hero backgrounds, gradient text, sleek Stripe-style minimalism, Linear/Vercel monoculture, AI-styled landing pages.

### Color & Theme

**Brand color: keep `#9b2324` (Arci red), evolve everything around it.**

The red is the soul — it ties to the Casa del Popolo / Arci heritage and is non-negotiable as the brand anchor. But it's an **accent**, not a flood: think 60% warm paper-cream / 30% ink-brown / 10% red. The current Material indigo-pink theme has to go entirely.

Construct the palette in OKLCH. Tint the neutrals toward the red hue (chroma ~0.005–0.01) so cream and ink feel related to the brand color rather than lifted from a generic neutral set. Consider one unexpected supporting accent (warm ochre, deep olive, or aged teal) for tertiary states — but use it like a second-page color in a Riso print, not as a competing brand color.

**Both themes, system-driven.** Implement light *and* dark, gated on `prefers-color-scheme`. The dark variant is *not* an inversion: it's the same room with the lights down — warm dark brown surfaces (not blue-black), preserved red, parchment-ivory text. A dark mode that feels generic is a failure even if it technically works.

### Design Principles

1. **Lower the threshold.** Every component is an invitation, not an interface. The newcomer who lands here should feel — within five seconds, before reading any copy — that they would be welcome on a Monday night.
2. **Hand-touched over machine-perfect.** Tiny imperfections, organic accents, asymmetric layouts, real photography of the actual space and the actual games. The site should look like it could plausibly have been made by the volunteers who run the club, not by an agency.
3. **Arci red is a punctuation mark, not wallpaper.** The red appears where it matters — and is rare enough that it still means something each time.
4. **Italian-made, not translated.** Design vocabulary is Italian community-cultural, not localized-American. References and anti-references both come from this lineage.
5. **Both themes, equally crafted.** Light is daytime paper; dark is evening felt-table. Neither is an afterthought.

### Accessibility

Italian community organizations attract a wide age range — including older members (Subbuteo crowd) who appreciate larger, calmer body type. Body text should be set generously (≥16px effective, line-height ≥1.55 on light, ≥1.6 on dark, max line ~70ch). Maintain WCAG AA contrast even with the warm palette — warm cream backgrounds can sneak below 4.5:1 against ink-brown if not checked. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. Hand-touched does not mean inaccessible.
